\chapter*{\centering ABSTRACT}

Quantum computing is rapidly advancing and poses a significant threat to traditional cryptographic systems that rely on computational complexity for security. While Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) provides a secure communication mechanism based on the principles of quantum mechanics, practical implementations remain vulnerable to environmental noise, hardware imperfections, and sophisticated eavesdropping attacks. This project presents an AI-Driven Threat Detection and Response framework that integrates BB84 Quantum Key Distribution, IBM Qiskit-based quantum simulation, and Machine Learning techniques to enhance communication security. The proposed system continuously monitors critical quantum communication parameters such as Quantum Bit Error Rate (QBER), noise level, sifted key length, and effective key rate to identify malicious activities. A Random Forest classifier trained on 50,000 simulated transmission records distinguishes intercept-resend attacks from normal channel disturbances with high accuracy. Upon threat detection, the system automatically initiates mitigation mechanisms including Privacy Amplification and migration to the E91 protocol, thereby reducing attacker knowledge and strengthening channel security. Furthermore, a real-time Security Operations Center (SOC) dashboard provides interactive monitoring, visualization, and analysis of quantum network conditions. By combining artificial intelligence with quantum cryptography, the proposed framework improves threat detection accuracy, response efficiency, communication resilience, and overall security for future quantum communication networks.
\vspace{0.5cm}

\noindent
\textbf{Keywords:} Quantum Cryptography, Quantum Key Distribution (QKD), BB84 Protocol, E91 Protocol, Quantum Bit Error Rate (QBER), Machine Learning, Random Forest Classifier, IBM Qiskit, Threat Detection, Intercept-Resend Attack, Privacy Amplification, Security Operations Center (SOC), Anomaly Detection, Quantum Communication Security, Automated Mitigation.

\chapter{Introduction}
The rapid advancement of quantum computing technologies has created significant challenges for conventional cryptographic systems that form the foundation of modern digital security. Traditional encryption algorithms such as RSA and Elliptic Curve Cryptography rely on computational complexity to protect sensitive information. However, the emergence of powerful quantum computers and quantum algorithms, particularly Shor's Algorithm, threatens to compromise these security mechanisms by solving complex mathematical problems exponentially faster than classical computers. As a result, there is an increasing need for next-generation security solutions capable of protecting communication systems against future quantum-enabled attacks.

Quantum Cryptography has emerged as a promising approach to address these challenges by leveraging the fundamental principles of quantum mechanics to provide secure communication. Quantum Key Distribution (QKD), particularly the BB84 protocol, enables two communicating parties to establish a shared secret key while ensuring that any unauthorized interception attempt introduces detectable disturbances into the quantum channel. Unlike traditional cryptographic methods, the security of QKD is based on physical laws such as the No-Cloning Theorem and Wave-Function Collapse rather than computational assumptions, making it resistant to attacks from both classical and quantum computers.

Despite its theoretical security advantages, practical implementations of QKD face several challenges. Environmental noise, transmission losses, hardware imperfections, and sophisticated eavesdropping attacks can affect the reliability and security of quantum communication channels. Distinguishing malicious activities from normal channel disturbances remains a critical challenge in real-world deployments. Conventional approaches often rely on fixed threshold values for metrics such as Quantum Bit Error Rate (QBER), which may result in inaccurate threat detection and increased false alarm rates.

\section{Introduction to Domain}

Quantum Cryptography is a rapidly emerging field of cybersecurity that utilizes the principles of quantum mechanics to provide secure communication and data protection. As digital communication continues to grow, the security of sensitive information has become a major concern for governments, industries, financial institutions, and individuals. Traditional cryptographic algorithms such as RSA and Elliptic Curve Cryptography rely on mathematical complexity to ensure security. However, the development of quantum computing technologies poses a serious threat to these conventional encryption methods, as quantum algorithms can potentially break them in a significantly shorter time than classical computers.

To address this challenge, Quantum Cryptography introduces security mechanisms based on the laws of physics rather than computational assumptions. One of the most widely used techniques in this domain is Quantum Key Distribution (QKD), which enables two parties to securely exchange cryptographic keys while simultaneously detecting any unauthorized interception attempts. The BB84 protocol, the first and most popular QKD protocol, uses quantum states of photons to establish secure communication channels.

Despite its strong theoretical security, practical quantum communication systems face several challenges, including environmental noise, transmission losses, hardware imperfections, and eavesdropping attacks. These factors can affect communication quality and make threat detection more complex. Consequently, the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) with Quantum Cryptography has gained significant attention. AI-driven techniques can analyze communication patterns, identify anomalies, distinguish malicious activities from normal channel disturbances, and support automated threat response mechanisms.

The domain of AI-enabled Quantum Cryptography represents an important advancement in next-generation cybersecurity, combining the security advantages of quantum communication with the intelligence of machine learning to build robust, adaptive, and future-ready communication systems.

\section{Techniques of Domain}

\begin{enumerate}

\item \textbf{Quantum Key Distribution (QKD)}

Quantum Key Distribution is a secure communication technique that uses the principles of quantum mechanics to exchange cryptographic keys. It enables the detection of eavesdropping attempts during key transmission and provides a high level of security for communication systems.

\item \textbf{BB84 Quantum Cryptographic Protocol}

The BB84 protocol is the first and most widely used QKD protocol. It utilizes quantum states of photons and different measurement bases to establish secure communication between two parties while detecting unauthorized interception attempts.

\item \textbf{Quantum Bit Error Rate (QBER) Analysis}

QBER is a critical security metric used to measure errors in quantum communication channels. An increase in QBER often indicates the presence of channel disturbances, hardware issues, or potential eavesdropping attacks.

\item \textbf{Machine Learning-Based Threat Detection}

Machine Learning algorithms analyze quantum communication data to identify abnormal patterns and detect malicious activities. In the proposed system, a Random Forest classifier is used to distinguish attacks from normal environmental noise.

\item \textbf{Feature Engineering}

Feature engineering techniques are applied to derive meaningful information from raw communication data. The custom heuristic feature, Eve Contribution, helps isolate attacker-induced disturbances from natural channel noise and improves detection accuracy.

\item \textbf{Real-Time Security Monitoring}

Real-time monitoring continuously tracks communication parameters such as QBER, noise level, key rate, and threat probability. This enables rapid identification of suspicious activities and supports proactive security management.

\item \textbf{Automated Threat Mitigation}

Automated mitigation techniques respond to detected threats without manual intervention. Privacy Amplification and E91 protocol migration are used to reduce attacker knowledge and strengthen communication security.

\end{enumerate}

\chapter{Literature Survey}

\section{Literature Survey}

The increasing threat posed by quantum computing has motivated researchers to explore advanced security mechanisms capable of protecting digital communications against future quantum-enabled attacks. Quantum Cryptography, particularly Quantum Key Distribution (QKD), has emerged as a promising solution due to its ability to provide security based on the principles of quantum mechanics rather than computational complexity. At the same time, Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) techniques have demonstrated significant success in cybersecurity applications through intelligent anomaly detection and automated threat response mechanisms.

Numerous studies have investigated the implementation of the BB84 Quantum Key Distribution protocol for secure key exchange. Researchers have shown that BB84 enables secure communication while providing the ability to detect eavesdropping attempts through the analysis of Quantum Bit Error Rate (QBER). However, practical implementations of QKD are often affected by environmental noise, transmission losses, and hardware imperfections, making accurate threat detection a challenging task.

Recent research has focused on integrating Machine Learning algorithms with quantum communication systems to improve anomaly detection and security monitoring. Various classification techniques, including Random Forest, Support Vector Machines, and Neural Networks, have been used to identify malicious activities and distinguish attack-induced disturbances from normal channel noise. These intelligent approaches have demonstrated improved detection accuracy and reduced false alarm rates compared to traditional threshold-based methods.

Several studies have also explored automated mitigation strategies in quantum communication networks. Techniques such as Privacy Amplification, error correction, and advanced QKD protocols have been proposed to reduce attacker knowledge and enhance communication security. The findings from these studies indicate that combining Quantum Cryptography with Artificial Intelligence can significantly improve the reliability, adaptability, and effectiveness of next-generation cybersecurity systems.

\subsection{Survey 1}

\textbf{Bennett and Brassard (1984) -- Quantum Cryptography: Public Key Distribution and Coin Tossing}

This pioneering research introduced the BB84 protocol, the first practical Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) protocol. The authors demonstrated how quantum mechanics could be used to securely exchange cryptographic keys between two communicating parties. The protocol utilizes different quantum states and measurement bases to detect eavesdropping attempts during key transmission. The study established the foundation of modern quantum cryptography and demonstrated that communication security could be achieved through the laws of physics rather than computational assumptions.

\Needspace{6\baselineskip}

\subsection{Survey 2}

\textbf{Shor (1994) -- Algorithms for Quantum Computation: Discrete Logarithms and Factoring}

This research introduced Shor's Algorithm, a quantum algorithm capable of efficiently solving integer factorization and discrete logarithm problems. The study highlighted the vulnerability of widely used cryptographic algorithms such as RSA and Elliptic Curve Cryptography when confronted with powerful quantum computers. The findings emphasized the urgent need for quantum-resistant security solutions and accelerated research in Quantum Cryptography and Quantum Key Distribution technologies.

\Needspace{6\baselineskip}

\subsection{Survey 3}

\textbf{Buczak and Guven (2016) -- A Survey of Data Mining and Machine Learning Methods for Cyber Security Intrusion Detection}

The authors presented a comprehensive survey of Machine Learning techniques used for cybersecurity applications. The study analyzed various classification algorithms, including Random Forest, Decision Trees, Support Vector Machines, and Neural Networks, for detecting cyber threats and network intrusions. The research concluded that Machine Learning significantly improves threat detection accuracy and supports automated security monitoring. The findings provide strong motivation for integrating AI-based threat detection mechanisms into Quantum Cryptography systems to identify anomalies and eavesdropping attempts in real time.

\Needspace{6\baselineskip}
\subsection{Survey 4}

\textbf{Lo, Curty, and Tamaki (2014) -- Secure Quantum Key Distribution}

This paper discusses the practical implementation challenges and security aspects of Quantum Key Distribution systems. The authors analyzed various attack strategies against QKD networks and proposed methods to enhance communication security. The study emphasized the importance of monitoring Quantum Bit Error Rate (QBER) and implementing robust security mechanisms to identify malicious activities. The findings demonstrated that secure QKD implementations can provide long-term protection against both classical and quantum attacks.

\Needspace{6\baselineskip}

\subsection{Survey 5}

\textbf{Sarker (2021) -- Machine Learning: Algorithms, Real-World Applications and Research Directions}

This research presents a comprehensive overview of Machine Learning algorithms and their applications in intelligent decision-making systems. The study examined classification techniques such as Random Forest, Decision Trees, and Support Vector Machines for anomaly detection and predictive analytics. The findings indicated that Random Forest classifiers provide high accuracy, robustness, and reliability for cybersecurity applications. These results support the use of Machine Learning for intelligent threat detection in quantum communication networks.

\Needspace{6\baselineskip}

\subsection{Survey 6}

\textbf{Conti, Dehghantanha, Franke, and Watson (2018) -- Internet of Things Security and Forensics: Challenges and Opportunities}

This paper explores modern cybersecurity challenges and the growing need for intelligent threat detection systems. The authors discussed the role of Artificial Intelligence and data analytics in identifying cyberattacks, detecting anomalies, and automating security responses. The study concluded that AI-driven security frameworks significantly improve threat detection capabilities and reduce response times compared to traditional security approaches. The findings highlight the importance of integrating intelligent monitoring systems with advanced cryptographic techniques.

\section{Summary of Research Papers}

The literature survey indicates that Quantum Cryptography and Quantum Key Distribution provide highly secure communication mechanisms based on the principles of quantum mechanics. The reviewed studies demonstrate that protocols such as BB84 can effectively detect eavesdropping attempts and establish secure communication channels. However, practical quantum communication systems face challenges including environmental noise, transmission losses, and sophisticated attack strategies.

The surveyed research also highlights the growing importance of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning in cybersecurity applications. Machine Learning algorithms, particularly Random Forest classifiers, have demonstrated strong performance in anomaly detection, threat classification, and automated decision-making. Several studies have shown that integrating intelligent monitoring systems with secure communication frameworks significantly improves threat detection accuracy and response efficiency.

The findings suggest that combining Quantum Cryptography with Artificial Intelligence provides a promising approach for next-generation cybersecurity systems. However, existing solutions often focus either on quantum communication security or AI-based threat detection independently. Therefore, the proposed project aims to develop an integrated framework that combines BB84 Quantum Key Distribution, Machine Learning-based threat detection, real-time monitoring, and automated mitigation mechanisms to enhance communication security and resilience against future cyber threats.

\section{Need and Scope}

\subsection{Need of the Project}

The rapid advancement of quantum computing technologies has created significant challenges for traditional cryptographic systems that secure modern digital communications. Conventional encryption algorithms such as RSA and Elliptic Curve Cryptography rely on mathematical complexity for security; however, powerful quantum computers have the potential to break these algorithms efficiently. This emerging threat has created an urgent need for advanced security solutions capable of protecting sensitive information against both classical and quantum attacks.

Although Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) provides secure communication based on the principles of quantum mechanics, practical implementations face challenges such as environmental noise, transmission losses, hardware imperfections, and sophisticated eavesdropping attacks. Existing security approaches often rely on static threshold-based monitoring, which may lead to inaccurate threat detection and delayed response. Therefore, there is a need for an intelligent security framework that combines Quantum Cryptography with Artificial Intelligence to continuously monitor communication channels, accurately identify threats, and automatically initiate appropriate mitigation mechanisms. The proposed system addresses these challenges by integrating Machine Learning-based threat detection, real-time monitoring, and automated response mechanisms into a unified quantum security platform.

\subsection{Scope of the Project}

The scope of the proposed \textit{AI-Driven Threat Detection and Response Using Quantum Cryptography} system focuses on enhancing the security of quantum communication networks through the integration of Quantum Key Distribution and Artificial Intelligence. The system implements the BB84 protocol for secure key exchange and continuously monitors critical communication parameters such as Quantum Bit Error Rate (QBER), noise level, sifted key length, and effective key rate.

The project utilizes a Random Forest Machine Learning model to detect malicious activities, including intercept-resend attacks, by analyzing communication patterns and distinguishing attacker-induced disturbances from normal channel noise. The system also provides real-time visualization through a Security Operations Center (SOC) dashboard and supports automated mitigation mechanisms such as Privacy Amplification and E91 protocol migration.

The current scope of the project is limited to simulated quantum communication environments using IBM Qiskit and Machine Learning-based threat detection. Future enhancements may include deployment on real-world quantum communication infrastructures, integration of advanced AI models, support for additional QKD protocols, and development of large-scale quantum network security solutions.

\section{Algorithms}

The proposed \textit{AI-Driven Threat Detection and Response Using Quantum Cryptography} system utilizes Quantum Key Distribution, Machine Learning-based threat detection, real-time monitoring, and automated mitigation techniques to provide secure communication. The major algorithms used in the system are described below.

\subsection{BB84 Quantum Key Distribution Algorithm}

\textbf{Algorithm Steps:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Generate a random sequence of bits and corresponding quantum bases.
\item Encode each bit into a quantum state using the selected basis.
\item Transmit the quantum states through the quantum communication channel.
\item Receiver randomly selects measurement bases and measures incoming qubits.
\item Compare bases through a public channel and discard mismatched measurements.
\item Generate the sifted key using matching basis measurements.
\item Calculate the Quantum Bit Error Rate (QBER).
\item If QBER exceeds the threshold, declare a possible eavesdropping attack.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{QBER Calculation Algorithm}

\textbf{Algorithm Steps:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Collect transmitted and received key bits.
\item Compare corresponding bit values.
\item Count the total number of mismatched bits.
\item Calculate QBER using:
\[
QBER=\frac{\text{Number of Error Bits}}{\text{Total Compared Bits}}
\]
\item Forward the calculated QBER value for threat analysis.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Feature Engineering Algorithm}

\textbf{Algorithm Steps:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Collect communication parameters such as QBER, Noise Level, Sifted Key Length, and Effective Key Rate.
\item Process the collected data and remove inconsistencies.
\item Compute the Eve Contribution feature:
\[
EveContribution=\max(0,QBER-0.66\times NoiseLevel)
\]
\item Normalize and prepare features for Machine Learning analysis.
\item Store processed data for threat detection.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Random Forest Threat Detection Algorithm}

\textbf{Algorithm Steps:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Load the trained Random Forest model.
\item Receive processed communication features.
\item Pass feature values to the classifier.
\item Generate attack probability and classification results.
\item Identify whether communication is normal or under attack.
\item Send results to the monitoring dashboard.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Real-Time Monitoring Algorithm}

\textbf{Algorithm Steps:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Continuously collect communication metrics.
\item Update QBER, Noise Level, and Key Rate values.
\item Display real-time telemetry on the dashboard.
\item Monitor security indicators and threat probability.
\item Generate alerts when suspicious activities are detected.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Privacy Amplification Algorithm}

\textbf{Algorithm Steps:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Receive the shared secret key after threat detection.
\item Estimate potential attacker knowledge.
\item Apply privacy amplification functions.
\item Generate a shorter but more secure cryptographic key.
\item Replace the original key with the amplified secure key.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{E91 Protocol Migration Algorithm}

\textbf{Algorithm Steps:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Detect persistent or high-risk attacks.
\item Trigger protocol migration procedure.
\item Establish communication using the E91 protocol.
\item Verify secure key generation through entanglement-based communication.
\item Continue secure communication using the upgraded protocol.
\end{enumerate}

\item \textbf{Scalability:} The system should support increasing network traffic, users, and security events without significant performance degradation.
